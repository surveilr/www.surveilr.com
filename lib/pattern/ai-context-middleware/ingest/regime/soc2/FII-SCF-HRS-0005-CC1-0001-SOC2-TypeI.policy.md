## Introduction

The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework for communicating core values from executive management to personnel, ensuring that all employees understand and adhere to the organization's principles. This policy aligns with the requirements for effective governance and compliance, particularly in relation to the management of electronic protected health information (ePHI). Effective communication of core values is essential for fostering a positive organizational culture and promoting ethical behavior among employees.

## Policy Statement

It is the policy of [Organization Name] to ensure that core values are clearly communicated from executive management to all personnel through formal policies and the employee handbook. This communication will be achieved through a combination of machine attestable methods and human attestable methods to ensure comprehensive understanding and compliance across the organization.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who have access to any systems or applications that create, receive, maintain, or transmit ePHI. This includes, but is not limited to, cloud-hosted systems, Software as a Service (SaaS) applications, and any other channels used by the organization.

## Responsibilities

- **Executive Management**: 
  - **Communicate core values**: Ensure core values are articulated and updated **Quarterly**.
  
- **HR Department**: 
  - **Distribute employee handbook**: Provide access to the employee handbook, ensuring it includes core values. This action should occur **Annually**.
  
- **Compliance Officer**: 
  - **Monitor adherence**: Review compliance with core values and report findings **Semi-Annually**.

- **All Employees**: 
  - **Acknowledge core values**: Affirm understanding of the core values as part of the onboarding process and **Annually thereafter**.

## Evidence Collection Methods

1. **REQUIREMENT**: Communication of core values must be documented and accessible to all employees.
   
2. **MACHINE ATTESTATION**: 
   - Utilize OSquery to generate reports on employee access to the employee handbook and confirm acknowledgment of core values.
   - Implement API integrations to track distribution and updates to the employee handbook.

3. **HUMAN ATTESTATION**: 
   - Employees must sign a form acknowledging their understanding of core values during onboarding and at least once per year. 
   - Maintain records of these acknowledgments in the HR system for verification purposes.

## Verification Criteria

Compliance with this policy will be measured by the following **KPIs/SLAs**:
- **100%** of new employees must acknowledge core values within **30 days** of hire.
- **95%** of existing employees must reaffirm their understanding of core values at least **Annually**.

## Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy may be granted under specific circumstances, such as temporary inability to access the employee handbook. All exceptions must be formally documented and require written approval from the Compliance Officer. Exceptions must be reviewed and determined within **5 business days** of the request.

## Lifecycle Requirements

Evidence and logs related to this policy must adhere to the following **Data Retention** guidelines:
- All acknowledgment forms must be retained for a minimum of **7 years**.
- Policy reviews and updates must occur **Annually** to ensure continued relevance and compliance.

## Formal Documentation and Audit

All workforce members must acknowledge their understanding and compliance with this policy through signed documentation. Comprehensive audit logs must be maintained for all critical actions related to the communication of core values. Any exceptions must be formally documented and reviewed during the **Annual Review** of this policy.
